Descripción

Ubicado en un acantilado en 10 acres de terrenos subtropicales con vista al Atlántico, este complejo de 5 estrellas cuenta con un spa frente al mar, 3 piscinas y acceso directo a las aguas oceánicas.

Todas las habitaciones del Reid's Palace están elegantemente amuebladas e incluyen balcón y baño de mármol con artículos de aseo. Un minibar con bebidas de cortesía y películas gratis en la habitación están disponibles para cada habitación.

Reid’s Palace Spa cuenta con suites de terapia interiores y exteriores con hidromasaje privado. Ofrece un menú indulgente de tratamientos con marcas de spa de clase mundial. Saunas secas y húmedas también están en el sitio.

Los huéspedes activos pueden jugar un partido en las canchas de tenis o ponerse en forma con equipo cardiovascular en el moderno gimnasio.

Brisa Do Mar, de inspiración francesa, ofrece comidas al aire libre, mientras que la Villa Cipriani sirve platos clásicos italianos. El comedor organiza veladas elegantes en un ambiente eduardiano.

El Reid está a 3,2 km de la playa de Praia Formosa y a 15 minutos a pie del centro de Funchal, donde hay mucha vida nocturna. Ofrece aparcamiento privado gratuito.

Sao Martinho es una gran opción para los viajeros interesados en la relajación, la naturaleza y los paseos por la naturaleza.

Experiencias

02/09/2017

All time favorite...

"Wow! What a place! I stay at a lot of hotels and this has to be my all time favorite.The thing that this place has is character. So many five star hotels try to make things more and more comfortable, more and more caring. However so often they end up rather vanilla - something you can't find fault with, rather than something you love itself.The hotel is certainly comfortable and luxurious but more than that it is its own place. From our balcony we have views over amazing gardens, these lead down to pools, then down cliff steps to a sea platform. Two diving boards, a small sea pool and clear water of between three and six meters in depth. It is a fabulous place for messing around; snorkelling with the fish, diving off boards, visiting the floating island.The kids loved the pools and the sea and the free mini-bar - they are easily pleased. However even I was unreasonably charmed by the staff who brought grapes and ice creams for those lounging around by the pool.Yes it's quite posh but it's old money posh not new money bling. In the many historic pictures adorning the walls I find one from The Sketch,"Lady Prudence Jellicoe in mid air, the swallow dive".It appears Lady Prudence was quite a daredevil because she's diving off the cliffs from about ten meters up. Just because you're posh doesn't mean you can't be daring and fun. It's that kind of posh.The staff are a nice mix of young and old. The younger ones are energetic and attentive much like you find in many hotels. The older sometimes more butler than staff - helpful and knowlegable but occasionally indulging in a little bit of looking over the glasses. Which is actually quite charming.If you liked the movie "The Grand Hotel Budapest" this is your kind of place. Make a point of visiting the old entranceway and reception which is a wonderful wooden construction with cubbyholes for all the rooms."
