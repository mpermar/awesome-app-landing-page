Descripción

Todas las habitaciones disponen de aire acondicionado, zona de estar y TV de pantalla plana con canales por cable. Cada uno tiene un baño con ducha y artículos de aseo gratuitos. Algunas habitaciones tienen bañera.

El comedor Sirin sirve cocina tailandesa para el desayuno, el almuerzo y la cena.

El Casa Nithra Hotel se encuentra a 7 km de Patpong y a 36 km del aeropuerto de Suvarnabhumi. El establecimiento ofrece aparcamiento gratuito.

31/03/2017

Fantastic pool, attentive staff and good location

"Everything about the hotel rocked. We were close enough to the things we wanted to visit in Bangkok to walk to about half of them. The staff was very friendly and accommodating, to the extent that I almost hated to ask for anything because they acted like they had failed for not anticipating our desires.We only ate breakfast in the hotel restaurant on about half the days we were here. It wasn't because it wasn't good but because we wanted to experience more variety. I like a continental breakfast and everything including the croissants was fresh and high quality. I liked the variety of fresh fruit. My wife usually ate the full breakfast and there was an abundance of choices, American and Thai.Bangkok is hot and we are from a very temperate climate so our routine was to go out early to explore, come back to the room in the middle of the day and then go out at night. My wife usually napped (sleeping is her super power) and I went to the pool.Their roof top pool is fabulous. You can sit on lounges in a foot of water, order cold drinks from the bar and look out over the roofs of Bangkok. Everynow and then you slide off the lounge chair into the pool, cool off and then have another drink.In the evenings we would usually walk to Khao San road and Soi Ram Buttri to find dinner and watch the crazy kids. If you're there stop by Fu Bar and see if the band is any good. They had a kick ass Asian Beatles cover band one night that really rocked. Other nights not so much. Also my wife found a fantastic Tom Yam Kung at the Tom Yam Kung restaurant (odd coincidence there).Walking to Khao San the journey is as good as the destination. Samsen road is shops, street food, massage parlors (150 Baht for a half hour foot massage, my wife almost stayed in Bangkok) and, not to forget one of the most useful establishments in Bangkok, 7-elevens."
