Descripción

La isla presenta edificios coloniales tempranos, un faro histórico y varios túneles subterráneos para explorar. Las opciones de alojamiento incluyen villas independientes, dormitorios y cabañas básicas.

Con una de las escapadas más singulares de la isla de Australia Occidental, la oficina de registro de la Autoridad de la Isla Rottnest se encuentra a solo 2 minutos a pie del embarcadero principal de la isla Rottnest, que ofrece servicios de ferry desde y hacia Fremantle y Perth. El ferry a la isla dura aproximadamente 30 minutos.

Rodeada de cristalinas aguas azules y playas de arena blanca, la isla Rottnest tiene 7 millas de largo y 2.5 millas en su punto más ancho. Se encuentra a 12 millas al oeste de Perth.

- 01/05/2017 -

Cabins allow families to afford Rottnest - and get closer.

"Caroline Thomson cabins are perfect for groups and families looking to experience Rottnest without the huge expense of all the other accommodation on the island. The location of the cabins is unbelievably central and means if you don't want to hire bikes you don't really need to. Walk to shops, cafes, restaurants, or ride. Pinky's beach is just behind the cabins, the lighthouse too, Thomson Bay just over the rise. Amenities (bbq's, public toilets and grounds) were lovely and clean and we saw them being cleaned every single day of our stay. Top marks to the Rottnest Authority for staying on top of things. I gladly picked up disgusting cigarette butts that lazy smokers left lying on the ground around our cabin - if every guest did the same we could really keep the wildlife happy and keep Rottnest pristine. The mattresses are great - firm but comfortable, and with wipeable latex tops so you don't feel like there's other people's traces on them. The blankets are warm, but they're wool so they're itchy and you don't feel very "nice" with them on you because you know they mightn't have been washed recently. Pillows are good, but they're a personal thing too so I was uncomfortable for our entire stay. Suggest you don't forget to bring sheets, pillowcases, towels, soap, and your own sleeping bags and pillow too if you can. A fan would be nice in the cabins. We didn't need it for this stay but any warmer and you'd want one. They do have a small oil heater for winter.No TV and close quarters meant this normally device-reliant family was forced to spend time together and our night times were great fun, like camp-outs complete with scary stories and lots of laughs. Kids and mum and dad had a ball. I'll miss the beautiful quokkas and will book the cabins again because we can't afford the other accommodation. I recommend the cabins to everyone. Enjoy :)"
