Description

The modern guest rooms include a full kitchen and a spacious work desk at the Element DFW North. The rooms include paper recycling baskets and large windows, designed to let in natural light.

An evening reception with beverages and snacks is held near the lobby Monday through Thursday. Several popular restaurants are located within walking distance of the hotel.

The hotel provides guests with free access to its fitness center. The hotel also has a business center and a small store with snacks and toiletries.

The DFW Element is 5 miles from Irving CVB and provides easy access to Interstate 635. Grape Vine Mills Mall is a 7-minute drive from the hotel.

Broken AC but excellent hotel, value for money for sure!

"I arrived with my family consisting of 3 kids under the age of 3.5 and 2 adults. After a long flight and no nap nor food on the flight, the kids were already a wreck by the time we touched down at 7pm. Element's shuttle took 40min to arrive and pick us up even though I was told on the phone 20min! When we arrived at the hotel, they couldn't find my booking.com reservation! 45 min later amidst screaming kids running amok in the foyer, the receptionist was looking at transferring us to another hotel because Element was fully booked. Refusing to be reallocated in view of the tired and hungry children, I demanded for a room key, suggesting that they transfer another guest instead. When I arrived at the room, it was hot and stuffy because the AC was not turned on. After I turned it on, it failed to cool the room after an hour! The faulty AC was reported to Reception, who came to the room and informed me that he was trying to reset the system to see if it would work. Meanwhile, my husband pulled out an extremely clogged filter from the AC and brought it to Reception to be replaced. The receptionist did find us a replacement filter, so all of us retired for the night thinking the room would cool down. I awoke at 1am drenched in perspiration. As all the kids were sleeping, I could not do a room change and instead settled for an electric fan, which whirred noisily throughout the night until 5am, when my husband could not stand it anymore. He put it outside the room and left our door slightly ajar to blow cool air from the corridor into the room. As the bed was around the wall of the entrance, it took a long time to cool the room. I eventually ended up moving the sofa cushions to the front door, where I slept on the floor for the next 3 hours. When I complained to the same receptionist who checked me in the night before, he did not hesitate to check with the manager to accord me a discount in compensation for the discomfort all night and hassle."
