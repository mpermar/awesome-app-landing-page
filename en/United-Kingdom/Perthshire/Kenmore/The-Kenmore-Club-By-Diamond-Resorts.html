Description

With a magnificent location on the banks of Loch Tay, close to the picturesque village of Kenmore, these charming and well-appointed cottages offer luxurious self-catering accommodation.

Free WiFi is available throughout the property. The Kenmore Club was originally the walled garden for the nearby Taymouth Castle, home of the Marquis of Breadalbane.

Today, this small, exclusive resort consists of a series of cottages, constructed in the same unique style as the nearby village of Kenmore.

The resort includes a swimming pool, a tennis court and a children's playground, as well as a restaurant.

Each cottage has a large lounge, with comfortable chairs, a TV and a large put-up bed.

Every cottage also has a dining area and well-equipped kitchen, with a coffee maker, dishwasher, fridge, microwave and oven.

Some cottages have a washing machine and drying area. Just across the way, you will find Scotland's oldest inn, with a chimney breast inscribed by the hand of Robbie Burns himself.
