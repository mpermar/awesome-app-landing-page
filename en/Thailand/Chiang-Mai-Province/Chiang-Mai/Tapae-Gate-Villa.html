Description

At the hotel, each room includes a closet and a flat-screen TV. Every room has a private bathroom with a bath, free toiletries and a hair dryer. The rooms will provide guests with a fridge.

A continental breakfast is served each morning at the property.

Speaking English and Thai, staff are ready to help around the clock at the reception.

Tha Pae Gate is 600 feet from Tapae Gate Villa, while Three Kings Monument is 2,100 feet away. The nearest airport is Chiang Mai Airport, 2.5 miles from the property.

This is a brand new hotel, even the Tuk Tuk drivers...

"This is a brand new hotel, even the Tuk Tuk drivers haven't heard ofit yet!! Therefore, all the staff are super nice and attentive, letshope their great service remains the same after a year or so! This isonly a small hotel and quite quirky, such as the very small doors to theroom, my wife took great delight in watching me squeeze through everytime (you can open both doors to make it easier). Our pool room wasimmediately after reception but to be honest we didn't suffer anyreal noise and it was lovely to step outside the back door and straightinto the pool!! Young families should just be aware of this, the poolreally is outside your back door!! The swimming pool is lovely,thankfully it was warm enough to swim in, its not huge but for the typeof location and size of space available, it was perfect. We were theonly family using it and it felt as though it was our own private pool,my son loved it. The room itself was fine, big bed, although its astrange construction, you have to crawl over a large wooden'shelf' to get in and out, it might cause some problems if youare not the most mobile of people. A very nice big bath acts as yourheadboard and there is an additional shower inside the main bathroom.The 'free' mini bar is a nice touch, some crisps, nuts,chocolate and soft drinks available and quite unique, it was brieflymentioned by the receptionist, so I'm guessing not many guests takeadvantage as they may not be 100% sure that its free! Breakfast is fine,a similar choice every day but it is plentiful, if I could changeanything, i'd like the chef to offer omelets, he doesn't standby the breakfast area and I suspect he has limited facilities as theonly eggs on offer were fried. One very nice touch was every morning oneof the staff would bring me the Bangkok Post to my table, it was verymuch appreciated and I might have been the only English speaker therebut it was good service."
