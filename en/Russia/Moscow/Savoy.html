Description

The hotel has guest rooms and suites with orthopedic mattresses and anti-allergic pillows and free Wi-Fi. The marble bathrooms have floor heating.

You can swim in the pool and relax in the sauna. Spend several pleasant hours in the beauty salon.

Guests can taste excellent local and European dishes in Savoy restaurant.

At the 24-hour front desk, you can get information about tours and tickets. The friendly and professional staff speaks English.

Meshchansky is a great choice for travelers interested in culture, history and museums.

I booked three days and ended up staying for 9.

"I booked three days and ended up staying for 9. The hotel is superb. The location can't be better - 5 minutes walk to Red Square, Bolshoi Theater, 3 lines of Metro are just couple of minutes away. Very secure area even late at night with police guards around the area with its government offices. Fantastic supermarket and a mini shopping center with everything one might desire - from medicines to kids toys just across the street (Azbuka Vkusa). I had both Classic and Executive room. Executive is a bit more spacious and lush - but Classic is definitely not "inferior" as with many hotel chains. Rooms are quiet with double windows and blackout curtains. Bed and bedding are decidedly luxurious if understated ( no frilly pillows or heavy satin covers - just 100% white linens - no smells, no allergy red flags). Super nice well sized bathroom with shower, huge bath and bidet. British shampoo/lotion etc... WiFi worked flawlessly. Breakfast is highly recommended. Includes Champagne even on workdays:) Buffet with big selection - from fresh fruits and seasonal veggies to sumptuous smoked fish ( highly recommended - not "Norwegian" fake salmon - but real Russian stuff. Excellent coffee - even straight from the machine. Real samovar with fantastic selection of teas. Fresh squeezed orange juice. Cereals from muesli to buckwheat kashi. Yoghurts ( western made) , local keffir and cottage cheese. The staff is multilingual and very professional. I witnessed the "handling" of a rowdy Irish tourist ( sorry, no offense meant - just stating the fact) who appeared at the breakfast with her cell phone, talked and swore loudly - and demanded to have a doggie bag for buffet breakfast. The waiting staff didn't even roll their eyes as we did. It was in the end up to me to come up to this woman and ask her to tone down her voice and demands."
