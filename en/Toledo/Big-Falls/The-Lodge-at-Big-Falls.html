02/12/2016

We stayed here 4 nights, after spending a couple weeks up...

"We stayed here 4 nights, after spending a couple weeks up north in the Orange Walk area, and found this to be a wonderful base for birding in less-visited southern Belize (with Steven, a great local guide). A small river and broad-leaf forest surrounds the property, with wildlife (like river otters and agouti), and numerous birds--we saw probably 75 species just on (or above) the lodge grounds, and many more in the general vicinity during day trips. Very flexible birding options are possible, including nearby national parks, or a trip north to the remnant scarlet macaw population (the only one left in Belize) if you're interested. There are ample cultural opportunities, too, but we didn't have time for much of that this visit. The lodge had very nice accommodation, excellent meals (best steak filet I had in Belize, plus fresh fish options, local vegetables/fruit, good coffee), and a quiet location. We had a new cabana with front porch and air conditioning (which actually wasn't needed during our visit, but would be good to have in hotter weather), including hardwood floors and interior, excellent tiled bath, comfortable beds, good lighting, and lots of room. (We didn't bring kids along, but this would be a fine lodge for a small family looking for a peaceful setting that would still be entertaining for children who like nature, canoeing, etc.) We'd definitely go back."
