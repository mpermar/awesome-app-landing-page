Description

The guest rooms at the Chateau Marmont are furnished with an iPod docking station, CD and DVD player. Most rooms provide a view of the surrounding gardens and select suites boast a full kitchen.

Bar Marmont uses local produce in each dish and also serves drinks and cocktails. Room service is available 24-hours a day.

For guest’s convenience, Marmont Chateau features a CD and DVD library. Guests may also use the gym and the hotel can arrange in-room massages.

The Hollywood Walk of Fame is 1.9 miles from Chateau Marmont Hotel. Grauman’s Chinese Theatre is 1.8 miles away and the hotel is 6.1 miles from Universal Studios.

Truly unique place. Relaxed and somewhat not like a hotel. More like staying at a friend's home.

"Staff were all excellent. I've read some complaints about them being aloof or inattentive, but I disagree with this assessment. They aren't being aloof, they are trying to be unobtrusive. If you dine at the restaurant will they check on you every 5 minutes? No, but a glance in your server's direction will have them at your table in moments. If you ask for something, you will get it but the place has just a different, more relaxed style. Much of the other clientele I saw was European so expect that type of atmosphere. Speaking of relaxed, the place is still a normal hotel. You can see people on vacation in shorts and flip-flops, it's just here, the next table will be in black tie and just come from the Emmy's. One thing you will not see is alot of children, which some may see as a positive. It's just not some place I could ever have imagined my parents taking me when I was a kid. Not that they'd be unwelcome, it's just not that kinda place.As for the bedroom, it was clean. I remember we were pleasantly surprised. It's not fancy or anything, but the carpet didn't have any stains on it, the linens were fresh and it all smells good. I guess they pump that Alessandra in the vents or something. Now, it was small, but more on that later. The feeling though, when you go in, is that it's a home. Like it doesn't feel so much like a hotel room, but more like you're visiting your aunt with a really large house.I was on the sixth floor on the East side in room 62, which is a standard room. The standout of that room though is the balcony, which literally is probably bigger than than the actual bedroom is area-wise. The view is out over Sunset, down at the Chateau Marmont sign, out towards downtown LA and is amazing. You know that LA view, the one in magazines, that every hotel in LA has on it's website? THIS is that view."
