The hotel is superbly situated literally 50 meters from the...

"The hotel is superbly situated literally 50 meters from the gondola station on the Jardin Alpin run (G3).This does mean you are quite a long distance from 1850 and are somewhat trapped in the hotel at night.The rooms are clean and the bathrooms fairly new with ample hot water, however, storage space is minimal and there are no heated towel rails or radiators for easy drying of ski gear.There are no tea or coffee making facilities and the TV is rubbish, fuzzy picture and hardly any channels.There are two restaurants the main one serving buffet style food for breakfast and dinner and a 'specialities' one that only serves Savoyarde dishes (Raclette or raw meat cooked on a hot plate) and has to be booked. The latter is small and if the hotel is busy, needs to be booked well in advance.The bar area is tidy and has a good view of the piste but the drinks are expensive and happy hour not much cheaper.Be careful of the welcoming notices in the lift and bar areas offering a tea party of pancakes and vin chaud after skiing from 4.30-5.30, as this is not free and each little pancake is 5 euros, as is a cup of tea.The food in the restaurant seems good the first night, lots of salad, cold meats and cheese in the buffet area with a selection of desserts, but these do not change all week. The hot meal varies daily and on day one was duck (very good) with a fish alternative but the quality dropped rapidly the next day and for the remainder of the week. Children can have something off the a la Carte 'menu enfant' free of charge, but adults need to pay if they want to deviate from the poor quality fodder on offer in the buffet. The hotel was full, so lots of families with small children running around and acting as though all the communal areas were a play park. You had to fight to get a table (the restaurant clearly does not accommodate a full hotel, so needs to do several sittings and was massively understaffed."
