Description

As a member of Swiss Deluxe hotels, all of the elegantly furnished and luxuriously equipped rooms feature wired and wireless internet access. The rooms either look out onto the mountains and glacier or the courtyard and village.

The Grand Restaurant (dress code: jacket or dark suit), where half-board dinners and breakfast are served by a friendly hotel staff, is decorated in an elegant neo-Baroque style. It serves traditional Swiss and international dishes. The more informal Kronenstübli gourmet restaurant (dress code: smart casual) currently has 17 Gault Millau points and 1 Michelin star. Guests can also enjoy light menus and drinks on the wooden terrace of Le Pavillon. The Grand Hotel Kronenhof was awarded GaultMillau Hotel of the Year in 2008. The wide range of restaurants guarantees gourmet variety.

The Kronenhof Spa is over 21,528 ft² and features a lavish pool, a children's pool, a wellness bath area, a Finnish sauna, a bio sauna, a stone grotto steam bath, a saltwater grotto, a relax floating-bath, a Kneipp footpath, a relaxation room with fireplace, 13 treatment rooms, as well as a fitness center with state-of-the-art equipment.

For children older than 3 years old, the hotel has its very own children’s club and children's restaurant. Professional babysitting services are provided. During the summer, children up to 12 years old stay free of charge in an extra bed.

Reduced prices for ski passes are available at the hotel during the winter for guests staying 2 or more nights. During the summer, guests staying 2 or more nights receive free use of all mountain trains in the Upper Engadine region.
